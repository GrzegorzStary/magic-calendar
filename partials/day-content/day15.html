International Tea Day

International Tea Day, celebrated on December 15th, invites tea enthusiasts to enjoy the world's most popular beverage while raising awareness about the tea trade's impact on farmers and workers. With origins tracing back to ancient China, tea has evolved from a medicinal drink to a beloved global staple, and this day encourages us to appreciate its cultural significance and support fair practices in the industry.

How to Celebrate International Tea Day
  • Try a New Flavor: Explore a variety of tea blends, from refreshing mint to fruity apple, and discover new favourites!
  • Host a Tea Party: Gather friends for an enjoyable tea party, complete with different blends and perhaps a vintage English theme.
  • Learn About Local Producers: Take some time to understand how your favourite teas are sourced and produced, and consider switching to brands that prioritise fair treatment for their workers.
Why International Tea Day Matters

Tea is steeped in history and culture, with unique traditions surrounding its consumption across the globe. Supporting the tea industry is vital, as many rely on it for their livelihoods; advocating for fair treatment of workers ensures a sustainable future for this cherished beverage.
